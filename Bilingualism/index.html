Module overview

The goal of this module is for you to get familiar with basic concepts and methods for researching how people understand the sounds of speech. By the end of the module, hopefully you will be able to do the following things:

  • Know the two competing theoretical ideas often debated in bilingualism research;
  • Know what 'interlingual homophones' are;

Module instructions

This module is still being built. For now, it just includes two tasks. For each task, you will need to do something or read something, and then answer some questions. Some of these questions may require a long time to think about. You should do the tasks in the order they are presented here, because each task builds off of the previous one. 

Next to each task I have written an estimate of how much time you might need to complete the task. This is, of course, a rough estimate, and the real time may be different for different students.

Module activities

  1. Complete two experiments online (15 minutes) [COMPLETE FOR CLASS]
  2. Thinking about the experiments (1 hour) [COMPLETE FOR CLASS]
  3. Interlingual homographs (under contstruction)
