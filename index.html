Overview

This site will contain most of the course content for CBS592 Psycholinguistics. Each week, the site will be updated with readings and activities that you should complete before the next class period. However, some content is already available in case you're interested to get going.

  • Modules. The class content will be divided into modules (basically units or sections). This is an active class; a module consists not just of readings or lectures, but also of activities that you must complete. Some modules are broken into multiple steps, such that later steps are intended to be "unlocked" after you submit your results from doing the earlier tasks. Here's the schedule of the modules for the class. They will become 'active' as the site is updated each week.
    • Introduction to psycholinguistics (completed in class)
    • Psychological mechanisms 
    • Priming 
    • Building a priming experiment  
    • Speech sounds 
    • Sentence comprehension 
    • Eye-tracking 
    • Neurolinguistics 
    • Child language acquisition 
    • Second Language Acquisition 
    • Bilingualism 
    • The Linguistic Relativity Debate & Review
